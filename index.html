📋 IT Compliance Policy

1. Company Information Security Policy

This policy outlines the mandatory guidelines for IT and Information Security across the organization. All employees are required to adhere to these standards to ensure data protection and operational security.

2. Data Protection & Confidentiality

  • Confidentiality: All company data, client information, and proprietary information is strictly confidential. Do not share sensitive information with unauthorized personnel.
  • Data Access: Access data only when required for your job duties. Minimize data exposure and secure all documents containing sensitive information.
  • Data Storage: Store data in company-designated locations (shared drives, databases). Do NOT store company data on personal devices or unsecured cloud services.
  • Data Disposal: Properly dispose of confidential documents by shredding. Delete digital data securely when no longer needed.
  • Violations: Data breaches or unauthorized access will result in disciplinary action up to and including termination.

3. Password Security

  • Password Requirements: Minimum 12 characters with uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and special symbols.
  • Password Management: Never share passwords with colleagues. Use a company-approved password manager. Never write passwords on paper.
  • Password Changes: Change passwords every 90 days. Do not reuse previous passwords.
  • Compromised Passwords: If you believe your password has been compromised, change it immediately and report to IT department.

4. Device & Network Security

  • Company Devices: Company devices (laptops, desktops, phones) are for official use only. Personal use is strictly prohibited.
  • Software Updates: Keep all devices updated with latest security patches and antivirus software.
  • Remote Access: Always use company VPN when accessing company resources from outside the office.
  • Physical Security: Lock your device when away from your desk. Never leave devices unattended.
  • Phishing & Malware: Do not click suspicious links or download unknown attachments. Report suspicious emails to IT immediately.

5. USB Drives & External Devices

  • Only use company-approved USB drives and external devices.
  • Encrypt all external devices containing company data.
  • Report lost or stolen devices immediately to IT and security.

6. Internet & Email Usage

  • Use company email only for business purposes.
  • Do not access inappropriate websites from company network.
  • Be aware that company monitors internet and email usage for compliance.

Non-compliance with this policy will result in disciplinary action.

🔒 IT Compliance Requirements

Mandatory Security Requirements for All Employees

1. Security Awareness Training

  • Complete annual IT Security Awareness training within 30 days of joining.
  • Attend quarterly security updates and training sessions.
  • Stay informed about latest security threats and best practices.
  • Training completion is mandatory for all employees.

2. Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

  • Enable 2FA on all company accounts (email, VPN, cloud services).
  • Use authenticator apps (Google Authenticator, Microsoft Authenticator) or hardware tokens.
  • Do not use SMS for 2FA when possible (less secure).
  • Backup authentication codes must be stored securely.
  • 2FA is mandatory - no exceptions.

3. Password Management

  • Change password every 90 days minimum.
  • Maintain 12+ character passwords with complexity.
  • Never reuse old passwords.
  • Password expiry notifications will be sent - do not ignore.

4. Security Incident Reporting

  • Report any security incidents within 24 hours to IT department.
  • Include: what happened, when, which devices/data affected, potential impact.
  • Do NOT attempt to fix security issues yourself.
  • Report suspicious activities, not just confirmed incidents.
  • Early reporting helps prevent larger breaches.

5. Device Security Compliance

  • Enable device encryption (BitLocker for Windows, FileVault for Mac).
  • Install and maintain active antivirus software.
  • Enable firewall on all devices.
  • Keep OS and applications updated within 30 days of release.
  • Run monthly security scans.

6. Monthly Compliance Checks

  • IT performs monthly compliance audits on all devices.
  • Devices must meet security standards to remain connected to company network.
  • Non-compliant devices will be disconnected until remediated.
  • You will receive automated notifications of any non-compliance.

7. Mobile Device Security

  • Enable PIN/biometric lock on all mobile devices.
  • Install company mobile device management (MDM) software.
  • Do not jailbreak or root devices.
  • Keep OS and apps updated.

8. Compliance Violations

  • First violation: Written warning + mandatory retraining
  • Second violation: Suspension of system access
  • Third violation: Termination of employment

These requirements are non-negotiable and apply to ALL employees without exception.

📄 IT Compliance Documentation

Complete IT Compliance Documentation

All employees must read and understand the following IT policies and procedures:

1. Acceptable Use Policy (AUP)

  • Guidelines for appropriate use of company IT resources.
  • Prohibited activities: hacking, data theft, inappropriate content, gaming, streaming.
  • Bandwidth limitations and fair usage policies.
  • Personal use must not interfere with business operations.
  • Company has right to monitor all activities.

2. Remote Work Security

  • Work-from-home setup requirements: secure internet, private workspace, locked door.
  • VPN mandatory for all remote access to company systems.
  • Public WiFi usage strictly prohibited for company work.
  • Screen visibility: Do not work where others can see sensitive information.
  • Virtual meeting guidelines: Use professional backgrounds, enable camera/microphone security.
  • Incident reporting: Report any security issues at home office immediately.

3. Personal Device Policy (BYOD - Bring Your Own Device)

  • Personal devices may be used only with IT approval and MDM enrollment.
  • Company retains right to remote wipe device if security is compromised.
  • Separation of personal and company data is mandatory.
  • Company is not responsible for personal device damage.
  • Device must meet minimum security standards (updated OS, antivirus, etc.).

4. Backup & Data Recovery Procedures

  • All critical work must be backed up to company cloud storage automatically.
  • Do NOT rely on personal backups for company data.
  • Data recovery requests must go through IT department.
  • Company maintains backup copies for 90 days.
  • Email backups are retained for 1 year.

5. Software License Compliance

  • Use only licensed software approved by IT department.
  • Personal software licenses cannot be used for company work.
  • Software piracy is strictly prohibited and illegal.
  • IT will conduct quarterly software audits.
  • Violators face termination and potential legal action.

6. Asset Management

  • All devices are company property - employee is responsible for them.
  • Report lost or stolen devices within 24 hours.
  • Return all devices before leaving company.
  • Device serial numbers must be registered with IT.

7. Data Classification

  • Public: Information that can be shared externally without risk.
  • Internal: General company information not for external sharing.
  • Confidential: Sensitive company data - requires encryption and restricted access.
  • Restricted: Highly sensitive (financial, HR, legal) - maximum security required.

Understanding these policies is essential for protecting company data and your professional responsibility.

📋 HR & Compliance Documents

Human Resources & Compliance Policies

1. Code of Conduct

  • Professional Behavior: Treat all colleagues with respect and professionalism.
  • Conflict of Interest: Disclose any conflicts that may impact your work.
  • Gifts & Hospitality: Do not accept gifts that could influence professional judgment.
  • Confidentiality: Maintain confidentiality of company and client information even after employment ends.
  • Compliance: Comply with all laws and company policies.
  • Violations: Any violations will be investigated and may result in disciplinary action.

2. Anti-Harassment & Anti-Discrimination Policy

  • Zero Tolerance: Company has zero tolerance for harassment, bullying, or discrimination.
  • Protected Categories: No discrimination based on race, color, religion, sex, age, disability, nationality, or sexual orientation.
  • Sexual Harassment: Unwelcome sexual conduct, advances, or requests are strictly prohibited.
  • Reporting: Report any harassment to HR immediately. All reports are investigated confidentially.
  • Protection: Company will not tolerate retaliation against anyone reporting harassment.
  • Consequences: Confirmed violations result in disciplinary action up to termination.

3. Leave & Attendance Policy

  • Annual Leave: Entitled to X days per year, carryover limit Y days.
  • Sick Leave: Entitled to Z days per year for medical purposes.
  • Casual Leave: Available for personal needs, approval required.
  • Maternity Leave: As per government regulations and company policy.
  • Paternity Leave: As per company policy.
  • Attendance: Punctuality is expected. Excessive absenteeism may result in disciplinary action.
  • Notice Period: Provide advance notice for leave requests when possible.

4. Compensation & Benefits

  • Salary: Paid monthly on specified date.
  • Bonus: Annual bonus based on performance and company results.
  • Health Insurance: Comprehensive health coverage for employee and family.
  • Retirement Benefits: Provident fund contributions as per law.
  • Other Benefits: Festival bonuses, gratuity, leave encashment as applicable.
  • Performance Incentives: Based on KPIs and goals achievement.

5. Data Privacy & GDPR Compliance

  • Personal Data: Company collects only necessary personal data for employment purposes.
  • Data Protection: Personal data is protected with security measures and encryption.
  • Data Usage: Data is used only for authorized HR and business purposes.
  • Data Rights: Employees have rights to access, correct, and delete their personal data.
  • Data Breach: Any breach will be reported within 72 hours as per GDPR.
  • Third Party Sharing: Data is not shared with third parties without consent unless legally required.
  • Compliance: Company complies with all data privacy laws (GDPR, CCPA, local regulations).

6. Performance Management

  • Reviews: Annual performance reviews conducted to assess progress and goals.
  • Feedback: Regular feedback provided for continuous improvement.
  • Development: Training and development opportunities provided.
  • Appraisals: Performance impacts salary increases and promotions.

7. Grievance & Complaint Procedure

  • Reporting: Report grievances to direct manager or HR department.
  • Investigation: All complaints are investigated objectively and confidentially.
  • Resolution: Resolution target is 30 days from complaint date.
  • Appeal: Right to appeal if decision is unsatisfactory.
  • Protection: No retaliation for filing genuine complaints.

8. Workplace Health & Safety

  • Safety: Company maintains safe working conditions.
  • Hazard Reporting: Report any safety hazards immediately.
  • Ergonomics: Workstations set up ergonomically to prevent injuries.
  • Mental Health: Employee assistance program available for mental health support.

All policies are designed to create a safe, respectful, and compliant workplace for all employees.
